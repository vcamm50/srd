Founded by [Stonehell](Locations/Stonehell%20Prison.md) escapee's, the town is named for both the winds coming from the canyon and the silent whispers it carries with it.

A long time ago a group of prisoners found a hidden cave in the depths of Stonehell. Beaten down, starving, and nothing to lose the group took the chance and fled into the cave. They spent traversing the twists and turns of the tunnels but they eventually emerged from a hidden cave entrance into daylight and freedom. Looking to distance themselves from the prison, the traveled for many days until a suitable spot to rest and rehabilitate was found.

It is known that the tyrant king threw anyone suspected of being a traitor in his many prisons and Stonehell was specifically built to relieve the overcrowding. Many of the prisoners were educated and hard working people with nothing more than an opinion. This particular group of escapees were builders, farmers, and townspeople from far away towns with no hope of getting home. They recognized the land as a good location for a settlement. The forest provided hunting, a nearby river provided fishing, and both the river and a nearby road provided travel and visitors. 

When the escapees regaining their strength and health, they began building and created a prosperous little town.

The irony of it all was spared from the founding settlers. They traveled for days and over hundred miles to place as much distance between them and Stonehell. However, their escape took them in circles and their days of travel was merely miles as the crow flies. With Stonehell's hidden entrances and their unfamiliarity with the lands surrounding them, the founders never learned their town is only a handful of miles and less than day's travel away from their hell.
