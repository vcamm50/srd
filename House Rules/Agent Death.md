Should an agent die or otherwise be lost, their XP gained from defeating monsters are gone, that knowledge is non-transferable and went with them to the afterlife. However, XP gained from treasure will be retained from agent to agent so you won't have to start from 0.

Your new agent can be created from scratch or your representative's hireling can be promoted, your choice. 
- Choosing a new agent allows you to be anything you want within the regular creation rules. You can balance of the team's compliment of classes or change things up according to your whim. Choice, basically.
	- When making a new agent, use your representative's XP (returned treasure to date) as the starting XP for the agent.
	- This may result a level bump if enough treasure was returned to town. Hooray!
	- This way you won't have to start over at level 1.
- Promoting a [hireling](Hirelings.md) instead has the potential of starting with a more experienced, higher level agent depending on how long they've been a hireling. The downside is lack of choice - the hireling's class and stats cannot be changed. They are what they are.
	- When promoting a hireling, either use their existing XP and level or use the representative's XP as a starting point, whichever is higher.
	- As above, should the representative's XP result in a level increase, hooray!
